You have most probably come across posts which says AI is stupid because it couldn't answer questions like 'how many days in the week contains the word day in it' and AI would answer 'Saturday' and 'Sunday' or something else. Then the author of the post would laugh at it and call the AI model stupid. This reminds me of this quote

> "Everybody is a genius. But if you judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree", Albert Einstein

A large language model (LLM) doesn't see the text as humans do. It tokenizes the text and that is why it struggles with questions like 'how many days in the week contains the word day in it?' or "how many r's are there in the word 'strawberry'?". We have created a new kind of creature by the collective wisdom and data produced by us until now. This creature doesn't have to be good at everything humans are good at or it doesn't have to correctly answer basic questions like above in order to be called useful. They have strengths of their own and weaknesses of their own. 

AI will make good enough very easy but it will make great much harder.
